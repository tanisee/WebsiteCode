Culture and Language

Food

Argentinian cuisine is a cultural melting pot of different influences introduced by the Spanish and Italian during 19th and 20th centuries along with a blending of taste by the Spanish colonizers with the Indigenous people of Argentina. Argentina is heaven for anyone craving a flame-grill cooked meal. ‘Asado’, or barbecue, is the official dish of Argentina. From corn to steak with spicy and colored sauces, there is always something for everyone. Anyone up for some BBQ?

Clothing

Like most countries, Argentina has a mix of traditional and modern clothing. Floral patterns including the ceibo, their national flower, can be found in many of Argentina’s traditional clothes. Both men and women wear brightly-colored ponchos made of wool during the winters. Wide-brimmed hats are also part of Argentinian clothing and help protect against the sun. Since soccer is extremely popular in Argentina, team jerseys are common casual clothes.

Language

Spanish, or Español, is Argentina’s official language and is predominantly understood and spoken as a first, or second language by nearly all of the population. In fact, around 42 million people in Argentina speak Spanish as their native language. English is another important language in Argentina and is taught in most schools. Argentina is the only Latin American country characterized as "high aptitude" in English and was placed 15th globally in the year 2015 for the popularity of the English language in the country. There are also 15 different indigenous languages. spoken throughout Argentina

Holidays

Dia de los Muertos
Also know as Day of the Dead, Dia de los Muertos pays respect to one's ancestors. To celebrate, people set up an altar with photos of their ancestors along with candles, flowers, food, and more!
Independence Day
Celebrated on June 9th, this is the day Argentina got its independence from Spain in 1816. There are many pariotic speeches and parades that are held
National Sovereignty Day
National Sovereignty Day celebrates the anniversary of the Battle of Vuelta de Obligado.

History

Argentina originally had many different tribes settled in that area. In 1480, The Inca Empire invaded and conquered the land, marking the first empire in the land. Later, in 1516, there was the arrival of Spanish chroniclers in the expedition of Juan Díaz de Solís, the start of Spanish settlement. Many people weren't happy with this and demanded a change. With the declaration of independence on July 9, 1816, and the military defeat, led by Jose de San Martin, of the Spanish Empire in 1824, a state was formed in 1853–1861. Thus, the Argentine Republic was born.

Geography

Argentina has a variety of geography. From large plains, grasslands, deserts, the tundra, and forests, to enormous mountains, rivers, and miles of ocean shore.

Did You Know... Argentina is the 8th largest country in the world! It occupies an area larger than Mexico and Texas combined

Argentina is at the bottom of South America bordering Chile to the south and west, Bolivia and Paraguay to the north, and Brazil, Uruguay,Chile to the south and west, and the Atlantic Ocean to the east. In fact, the Atlantic coastline is over 2,900 miles! Argentina also includes a part of Antarctica and islands in the South Atlantic.

Sports

In Argentina, almost everyone is a soccer fan, or a fútbol fan, as the locals call it. The sport is so popular, in fact, that the capital city has 24 teams alone! The famous footballer, Lionel Messi, was born in Rosario, Argentina. As recognized as this sport is in Argentina, it is not the official sport. The offical sport of Argentina is "pato", which litterally translates to duck in English. However, this game is not about ducks (anymore), but rather a combination of basketball and polo. People ride on horses while trying to get the balls into netted hoops.
